About Uncooked

The
Uncooked
Team.

In spring 2026, four climate practitioners got tired of climate content that only covered what had already worked and decided to build something different. Uncooked started as a podcast, grew into a sourcing letter, and is becoming a network: an ecosystem centered on students and early-career practitioners who need the kind of honest, research-grounded climate content that doesn't exist yet. We're building it as we go.

The Team Four co-founders · Berkeley + Columbia
B
Becca Pu
Co-Founder · Co-Host
MBA · Berkeley Haas  ·  S&P Global Climate Risk

Becca lives to funnel big money towards hard climate problems. She's spent the last few years building climate risk models for the World Bank and a climate tech startup, believing that quantifying the negative financial impact of climate change can push companies to make better decisions for the planet.

She also runs a sustainable fashion brand focused on upcycling ethnic minority textiles in rural China, and hopes to start a living-wage cooperative in that region. She holds a B.S. in Economics and Data Science from UC Berkeley and is pursuing her MBA at Berkeley Haas.

Anthony Rosato
Co-Founder · Co-Host
MS Sustainability Management · Columbia Climate School

Anthony is passionate about solving the climate crisis with innovation and better financing, drawing interest at the intersection of clean energy and sustainable finance. He's previously worked in private equity on portfolio company value creation and diligence within B2B SaaS, and most recently in a product role at an early-stage workforce education startup helping underrepresented, frontline employees build meaningful careers.

He holds a B.A. in Sustainable Environmental Design from UC Berkeley and is pursuing a Master of Science in Sustainability Management at Columbia University's Climate School.

Berkeley / Columbia Network

Gaby brings a cross-disciplinary lens to Uncooked, working at the intersection of climate science, community, and implementation. Her background spans both the research and practitioner sides of the climate space — exactly the gap Uncooked is built to bridge.
