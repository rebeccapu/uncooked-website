01 — Why We Exist

Most climate content is about solutions that have already won.

There's no shortage of podcasts about Tesla. No shortage of newsletters about solar. Plenty of conferences where the same VCs say the same things about the energy transition.

What's missing is coverage of the stuff that's still hard. The research that might not make it out of the lab. The founders building in sectors where there's no obvious business model yet.

Uncooked exists to close that gap. We dig into the darkest, dirtiest, hardest problems in climate — and the research and technologies trying to fix them. Before solutions are polished, proven, or mainstream.

"The most important climate work is happening in places most climate media never visits."

03 — How We Work

The Pairing is the format.

Every episode pairs one researcher with one founder working on the same hard problem.

  • The researcher brings rigor: what the science says, what the data shows, what the unknowns are.
  • The founder brings friction: what the market will pay, what regulation allows, what broke in the first pilot.
  • The conversation between them is where the interesting stuff happens.
"What the lab proves and what the market can bear are almost never the same thing. That gap is our subject."

20–25 minutes. A clear problem, two honest voices, and the courage to say what might not work.

04 — Who We Serve

Climate practitioners.
Not just enthusiasts.

We make Uncooked for people actively working in or building toward climate solutions. Our audience:

  • Early-career climate professionals understanding how research becomes reality
  • Graduate students at Berkeley, Columbia, Stanford, Yale considering founding a climate startup
  • Climate founders who need to understand the science in adjacent sectors
  • VCs who want early signal on what's coming out of labs before it shows up in a pitch deck
  • Professors who want to understand what the market needs from their research

We start at Berkeley and Columbia because that's where we are. We expand to Stanford and Yale because they have the same density of climate research and the same gap between the lab and the world.

Why we built this

We're two masters students — one at Berkeley Haas, one at Columbia — who couldn't find the climate show we wanted to listen to. Every podcast either covered tech that had already won or was so broad it said nothing useful.

We wanted a show about the hardest problems. About the research happening in the buildings we sit in. About the founders two years from a Series A who haven't been profiled yet.

So we made it. We start at Berkeley and Columbia. We expand from there. The goal is to be the go-to platform where climate research meets real-world execution — before solutions are polished, proven, or mainstream.
